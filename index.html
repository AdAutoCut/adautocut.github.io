Framework Overview

AutoCut employs dedicated encoders to extract video and audio features, then applies residual vector quantization to discretize them into unified tokens aligned with textual representations.

Figure: Overview of the AutoCut framework. Low-fps frames are tokenized for efficient multimodal reasoning, while high-fps frames are kept for accurate visual matching and clip retrieval.

Method

AutoCut unifies video, audio, and textual information within a shared discrete token space, enabling fine-grained multimodal reasoning and precise editing control.

🎬

Multimodal Encoder

CNN-based visual encoder (ResNet-50) extracts frame-level embeddings, while PANNs capture high-level audio features from each clip.

🔢

Tokenization

RQ-VAE discretizes continuous embeddings into unified tokens with a 256×8 codebook, enabling efficient compression and retrieval.

🎯

Multimodal Alignment

Built on Qwen3-8B, trained on ~700K samples to align video, audio, and text tokens in a shared semantic space.

⚡

Supervised Fine-tuning

Task-specific training on ~100K curated samples for video selection, ordering, script generation, and music selection.
